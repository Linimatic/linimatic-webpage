# Linimatic A/S — Brand Context

Essential facts and constraints for designing the Linimatic website. This skill provides the **what** and **why** — the designer decides the **how**.

## Company Facts

- **Name:** Linimatic A/S
- **Founded:** 1967
- **Location:** Helsinge, Denmark (~50km north of Copenhagen)
- **Specialty:** Denmark's largest dedicated zinc die-casting foundry
- **Employees:** 50+
- **Machines:** 12 hot-chamber die-casting machines (80-400 ton)
- **Capacity:** 12M+ parts per year
- **Materials:** Zamak 2, 3, and 5 zinc alloys
- **Certifications:** ISO 9001 (confirm IATF 16949 status)
- **Tolerances:** ±0.05mm
- **Markets:** Denmark, Scandinavia, Europe — B2B manufacturing
- **Languages:** Danish (primary), English (international), German (DACH market)

## Brand Positioning

Linimatic is **not the cheapest** zinc die-caster — and that is deliberate. The company positions as the premium partner for complex, quality-critical projects. The target customer has a difficult part, tight tolerances, or a project that another foundry struggled with. They are not comparing unit prices; they are looking for confidence that it will be done right.

**Company DNA:** "The best for the most complex"

**Competitive advantages:**
- **Complexity expertise** — Projects other foundries struggle with are Linimatic's specialty
- **Reliability** — Consistent delivery, dependable partner that procurement managers trust
- **Quality bar** — Premium output, tight tolerances, fewer rejects, minimal field returns
- **Skill level** — Deep expertise in challenging zinc die-casting, accumulated over 55+ years

**Messaging rules:**
- Never lead with price or cost savings
- Always lead with complexity, expertise, reliability, and quality
- Attract customers who have difficult projects, not price shoppers
- Use specific metrics (±0.05mm) rather than vague claims ("very precise")

## Brand Attributes

The website should communicate these qualities:

- **Precise** — Exacting tolerances, attention to detail, engineering rigor
- **Substantial** — Physical, grounded, material — this is a real factory making real things
- **Trusted** — 55+ years of continuous operation, established reputation
- **Nordic** — Scandinavian design sensibility — whatever that means to the designer
- **Expert** — Deep specialization in zinc, not a generalist

## Audience

| Audience | What They Care About | How They Arrive |
|----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Engineers | Technical specs, tolerances, alloy data, process details | Search for zinc/zamak/die-casting technical terms |
| Procurement managers | Capabilities, certifications, capacity, reliability | Search for suppliers, compare foundries |
| Decision makers | Credibility, track record, case studies, company stability | Referrals, LinkedIn, direct search |
| Product designers | DFM guidance, prototyping speed, design assistance | Search for zinc die-casting design resources |

## Design Constraints (hard requirements)

- **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- **Performance:** Server Components by default, optimized images, fast LCP
- **Multilingual:** All content in DA/EN/DE. German text is ~30% longer than English — layouts must accommodate this
- **Mobile-responsive:** Significant tablet traffic (factory floors, meeting rooms)
- **No stock photography feel:** This is a real company — design should feel authentic, not generic corporate

## Tone of Voice — Principles

- Technical authority without arrogance
- Concrete numbers over vague claims ("±0.05mm" not "extremely precise")
- Active voice, lead with key information
- Respect the audience's intelligence — they're engineers and buyers, not consumers

**Per language:**
- **Danish:** Professional but approachable, "du" form
- **English:** Clear international business English
- **German:** Formal "Sie" form, more thorough detail (German B2B expects depth)

## What This Skill Does NOT Prescribe

The following are creative decisions for the designer/agent to make:
- Color palette
- Font choices
- Layout patterns
- Visual style and aesthetic direction
- Animation and interaction patterns
- Specific component designs

These should emerge from the design process, informed by the brand attributes and company context above.
